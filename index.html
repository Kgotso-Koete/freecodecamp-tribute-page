Celebrating Black Woman Photographers in South Africa

A short collection

Noncedo Charmaine

Noncedo is a formidable photographer in her own right, and also one half of Carbon Copy, a photography collective with her twin.

Manyatsa Monyamane

Manyatsa Monyamane describes herself as an artist; storyteller through imagery, inspired and influenced by African literature.

Olefa Yalo

Olefa Yalo is a photographer, graphic designer, and a curator at She.Clix, “a female photography team that promotes emerging female photographers”.

Basetsana Maluleka

Basetsana is a self-taught freelancing photographer operating under the name Shutterbug Diaries. “The camera is my tool,” she says.

Fundiswa Ntoyi

Born and raised in Bloemfontein, before Fundiswa got into photography, she enjoyed sketching portraits but was always passionate about the craft of photography.

Boipelo Khunou

Boipelo Khunou is an artist, freelance photographer and videographer. She is currently studying towards her BA Fine Arts degree at the University of Witwatersrand.
